The Process

From raw metal
to finished
masterpiece

Each Template Watch passes through the hands of specialists whose combined training spans decades. No step is rushed, no detail overlooked. This is the process that earns the Poinçon de Genève.

01

Design & Engineering

Each new reference begins with months of sketching and CAD engineering at our Geneva manufacture. Our designers and engineers work in the same room, resolving aesthetic and mechanical constraints simultaneously — a practice that has defined Template Watches since 1887.

02

Movement Assembly

Under 40× magnification, a single master horologist assembles every component of the Calibre by hand. Springs are tensioned, jewels are pressed, and escapements are regulated over the course of several days. Each movement is signed by its maker before casing.

03

Case Finishing

Alternating satin and mirror polish surfaces are applied by hand. A single case takes 18 hours of polishing across multiple artisans, each responsible for a specific surface type. The contrast between the two finishes is what defines the Template silhouette.

04

Dial Decoration

Our dials are crafted in Geneva using guillochage, grand feu enamel, or hand-applied Super-LumiNova depending on the reference. Enamel dials are fired at 850 °C multiple times to achieve depth and vibrancy unavailable through any other technique.

05

Quality Control

Sixteen days of in-house testing. Every movement must pass COSC chronometer standards before casing, and then a further series of positional, thermal, and water-resistance tests specific to Template Watches' own benchmarks — which exceed COSC requirements.

06

Certification

Each piece receives the Poinçon de Genève seal and a signed certification card from its maker — a tradition dating to our founding. The seal is not applied to the movement until it meets every criterion of the Geneva Seal's eleven quality standards without exception.

In-House Philosophy

Nothing
leaves our hands
untouched

In an era of modular supply chains, Template Watches remains one of a small number of Geneva maisons that produces every component of its movements in-house — from the mainspring to the escapement wheel. We consider this not a marketing statement but an ethical commitment to the craft.

Our manufacture occupies three floors on the Rue de Rive, housing design studios, a CNC atelier, a hand-finishing room, and a climate-controlled assembly hall where temperature is held within 0.5 °C year-round. Every watchmaker has trained for at least eight years before touching a Template movement.
