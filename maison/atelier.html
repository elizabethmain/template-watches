14, Rue de Rive · Geneva

Our atelier occupies the same building on 14, Rue de Rive that Édouard Renard first opened in 1887. Three floors of workshop space house our movement assembly, dial decoration, and quality control departments. No piece leaves without the signature of its maker.

Every morning, our twelve master horologists arrive before eight o'clock. They work in silence, at benches that have been occupied by the same families for generations. The tools are old, deliberately so. A movement that has been assembled slowly, by hand, under 40× magnification, carries a precision that no automated line can replicate.

We produce no more than twelve completed watches per day. This is not a commercial constraint — it is an acknowledgement that quality cannot be hurried. When a piece is ready, the horologist who built it engraves their initials on the inner caseback. That signature is their promise to the owner: a lifetime of precision, accountability, and care.

The People

Our Master
Horologists

Pierre Dupont

Master Horologist

Thirty-four years at the bench, Pierre is our longest-serving horologist and the house's foremost authority on tourbillon assembly. He has trained every member of our current team and remains the only person permitted to sign off on Rhône Tourbillon movements.

Marie-Claire Voss

Dial Artist

A grand feu enamel specialist since 1998, Marie-Claire studied under the last masters of the Vallée de Joux before joining Template Watches. Each of her dials requires up to twelve firings at 800°C and may take three weeks to complete to her satisfaction.

Jean-Baptiste Arnaud

Head of Quality Control

A COSC certified examiner and former independent watchmaker, Jean-Baptiste oversees the final inspection of every piece. His department runs a movement through 16 positions over 15 days before clearance. Fewer than 3% pass on the first submission.

Isabelle Chervet

Enamel Artisan

Isabelle is the sole remaining grand feu artisan in the atelier who works exclusively in the cloisonné technique. Her waiting list for bespoke enamel commissions currently extends to 2027. Her work has been exhibited at the Musée d'Art et d'Histoire in Geneva.

Thomas Müller

Movement Engineer

Thomas joined from a leading Swiss ébauche manufacturer and was principally responsible for the design and development of Calibre TW-07T — our in-house flying tourbillon movement — which entered production in 2019 after six years of development.

Sylvie Renard

Case Finishing

The great-granddaughter of our founder Édouard Renard, Sylvie is a fourth-generation watchmaker whose family connection to the maison spans its entire 138-year history. She oversees all case anglage, brushing, and polishing, a process that can take up to eight hours per piece.
