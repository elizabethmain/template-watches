Our Heritage

A lineage of
uncompromising
precision

In 1887, master watchmaker Édouard Renard established our atelier in the heart of Geneva's Plainpalais district. Drawing on the city's centuries-long tradition of horology, he fused Calvinist precision with a distinctly Genevan artistry — a philosophy that every Template Watch carries to this day. With three apprentices, a lathe imported from Glashütte, and a singular conviction that a watch should outlast its owner, Renard created not merely a business but a standard of excellence that defined independent Swiss watchmaking for generations.


Through two world wars, economic upheaval, and the quartz crisis of the 1970s that decimated the Swiss watch industry, our atelier never wavered. Where others outsourced or mechanised, we deepened our commitment to hand assembly. Each calibre continued to be designed, engineered, and finished entirely within our workshops on Rue de Rive — the same cobbled street where Édouard first set his watchmaker's loupe to his eye. The house became a sanctuary for master horologists who believed that the worth of a timepiece resided not in its case metal, but in the invisible labour within.


Today, twelve master horologists carry that tradition forward. Each receives over 6,200 hours of training before their hands ever touch a production movement. They assemble no more than twelve pieces a day, each signed by the individual who built it. In an era of mass production, Template Watches remains stubbornly, defiantly handmade — not as a marketing proposition, but because Édouard Renard's founding conviction has never been superseded: that a watch made with absolute care for the person who will one day wind it is a different object entirely from any other.

Milestones

A century and a
half of precision

1887

The First Atelier

Édouard Renard opens at 14, Rue de Rive with three apprentices and a lathe from Glashütte.

1903

First In-House Calibre

Calibre ER-01, a pocket watch movement with 196 components, wins the Concours de Genève.

1924

Wristwatch Era

The house pivots to wristwatches, producing the first Lac Léman reference for the King of Belgium.

1962

Tourbillon Mastery

Master Horologist René Favre completes a 14-year project: the first Template Watches flying tourbillon.

1987

Centennial Collection

100 pieces celebrating a century. Every one sold within 48 hours at the Geneva Salon.

2019

Geneva Seal Certification

All movements receive the Poinçon de Genève, the strictest quality standard in watchmaking.

2025

A New Chapter

The current collection introduces three new references, each assembled by one of our twelve remaining master horologists.
