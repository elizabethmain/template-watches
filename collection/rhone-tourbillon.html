The Craft

The development of the Rhône Tourbillon spans fourteen years — a duration that reflects not indecision, but absolute refusal to compromise. The flying tourbillon cage, visible in its entirety at 6 o'clock, rotates once per minute and is suspended without an upper bridge, a feat that demands tolerances of less than two microns across every component. Our lead horologist, who oversaw the entire development, regards it as the definitive work of his career. The cage itself contains 73 individual parts, each weighing less than 0.01 grams.

The platinum case was chosen not merely for its rarity but for its specific acoustic and thermal properties, which protect the tourbillon from the micro-vibrations that afflict lesser materials. The skeletonisation process removes all non-essential material from the mainplate and bridges through a combination of CNC milling and hand-filing, then every exposed edge is hand-bevelled and polished to the Côtes de Genève standard. The result is a movement that appears to float within its case — an illusion so convincing that first-time observers instinctively reach to ensure the watch is actually running.
