What Is a Complication?

Every function beyond
telling the time

In horological terms, a complication is any function a watch performs beyond the simple display of hours and minutes. A date window is a complication. A chronograph is a complication. A tourbillon — which compensates for the effect of gravity on the escapement — is among the most demanding of all. The word comes from the Latin complicare: to fold together. Each additional function folds new layers of mechanical ingenuity into an already intricate system.

At Template Watches, we do not add complications for their own sake. Every complication in our collection serves a purpose that could not be served more simply. The flying tourbillon of the Rhône exists because we believe that a movement exposed to gravity deserves a mechanism that accounts for it. The column-wheel chronograph of the Mont Blanc exists because a measuring instrument demands an actuation system of absolute consistency. The grand feu enamel of the TW-22 is not a complication in the mechanical sense — it is a complication of a different order: the irreducible difficulty of making something beautiful by hand.

Together, these five references represent the full range of what our Geneva atelier produces. Each is assembled entirely in-house. None is produced in quantity. All are intended to be kept for a lifetime.
