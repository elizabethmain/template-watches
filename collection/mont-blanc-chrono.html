The Craft

The column-wheel chronograph mechanism of the TW-14C represents the gold standard of stopwatch engineering — a system of rotating columns that engage and disengage the start, stop, and reset functions with a tactile precision unmatched by the cam-and-lever systems found in lesser instruments. Where a cam-and-lever mechanism allows minor positional inconsistency, the column wheel locks each function into a defined position at every actuation, ensuring that the pusher buttons deliver an identical response whether the watch is worn by a racing driver or handled by a curator. The entire mechanism was designed over six years by a team of three engineers who came exclusively from the aviation instrument industry.

The grade 5 titanium case variant undergoes a finishing process unique to our atelier: after initial machining, the case is shot-blasted with ceramic media to create a fine, uniform matte texture, then selectively re-polished on the bezel flanks and lug facets by hand. The contrast between the satin surfaces and mirror-polished edges requires that each artisan work under 10× magnification to maintain a crisp, consistent boundary line. The steel variant follows an identical process, with the additional step of a micro-bead treatment on the dial side of the case to complement the matte anthracite dial.
