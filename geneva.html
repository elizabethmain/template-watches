Geneva & Horology

The world's
capital of
timekeeping

Geneva has been the world capital of precision timekeeping since the 16th century, when Huguenot craftsmen fleeing France brought their metalworking skills to the shores of Lac Léman. The city's particular culture of discipline, precision, and civic pride translated naturally into horology — and the watchmaking trade flourished.

By the 18th century, Geneva was producing more watches than any other city on earth. The Poinçon de Genève — the Geneva Seal — was established to protect and certify this legacy. Every Template Watch bearing the seal carries 500 years of accumulated knowledge, passed from master to apprentice across the rooftops of this extraordinary city.
